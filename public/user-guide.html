1. Overview

HKS Course Explorer aggregates Harvard Kennedy School course evaluation data (QReports) and bidding history into one searchable, visual tool. It is independently built and not affiliated with Harvard or HKS.

What data is included?

  • Evaluation scores — Student ratings across 12 dimensions (instructor quality, course rating, workload, rigor, readings, assignments, feedback, class discussions, diverse perspectives, discussion diversity, availability, insights) collected via end-of-term QReports.
  • Bidding history — Clearing prices, capacity, and bid counts per semester, sourced from HKS course selection records.
  • Faculty profiles — Instructor titles, categories, and aggregated multi-year ratings.
Important Evaluation data reflects past semesters. Instructors, course content, and bidding dynamics change. Use this tool to inform — not replace — your own research and conversations with current students.

Navigation

All six main sections are accessible from the navigation bar:

  • Home — Scatter plot overview of the full course catalog
  • Courses — Deep-dive into individual course detail, performance, and bidding
  • Faculty — Instructor profiles with aggregated ratings and teaching history
  • Compare — Side-by-side comparison of up to 5 courses across all metrics
  • Schedule Builder — Build a visual semester timetable, detect conflicts, export to iCal, and track plan credits
  • Requirements — Track degree requirements for all 5 HKS programs in real time against your plan
Desktop vs. Mobile Schedule Builder and Requirements are desktop-only tools — the timetable grid and multi-column card layouts require a large screen. On mobile, they redirect you to visit on a larger screen.

Navigation Modes

The app supports two navigation layouts:

  • Classic (default) — Left sidebar with branding, nav links, and HKS Resources. Toggle dark/light mode from the sidebar.
  • HUB Style — Full-width horizontal top navigation bar in Harvard institutional style. Click "🏛 HUB Style" at the bottom of the classic sidebar to switch. Click "← Classic View" in the top bar to return. See Section 8 for details.

2. Home — Scatter Plot & Course Cards

The Home page gives a bird's-eye view of the entire course catalog, with each dot representing one course offering.

Scatter Plot

The plot places courses on two axes simultaneously:

  • X-axis — Instructor Rating percentile: How this instructor ranks versus all HKS instructors in the dataset. Further right = higher-rated instructor.
  • Y-axis — Course Rating percentile: How the course itself is rated by students. Further up = higher overall course satisfaction.

This creates four natural quadrants:

QuadrantInterpretation
Top-rightGreat instructor and great course — the sweet spot.
Top-leftCourse content rated highly even if instructor rating is lower.
Bottom-rightWell-regarded instructor; course rating lags — possibly experimental or heavy.
Bottom-leftLower ratings on both axes — worth investigating why before enrolling.
Tip: Scatter Plot Interactions
  • Hover over a dot to see course name, instructor, concentration, and key metrics.
  • Click a dot to pin a detail panel below the plot — from there you can ⭐ star the course, ⇄ jump to Compare, or go to full Course Details.
  • Gold outlined dots — courses in your shortlist appear with a gold ring and larger dot, so you can spot them instantly even when browsing all courses.
  • Scroll/pinch to zoom in; use + / − buttons or drag to pan; double-click to reset.
  • Change axes using the X/Y dropdowns to plot any two metrics against each other (workload vs. rigor, instructor vs. course rating, bid price vs. any metric, etc.).

Preset Pills

Quick-filter buttons above the scatter plot let you surface specific course categories:

  • Top Rated — Courses with Instructor Rating ≥ 75th percentile
  • Light Workload — Sorts by lightest workload first (Workload percentile ascending)
  • STEM A / STEM B — Courses eligible for STEM designation (A = quantitative/technical, B = data/policy)
  • Core Courses — Required HKS core curriculum courses
  • Bidding 2026 — Switches to the active 2026 bidding season view, sorted by competitiveness
  • ★ Shortlist — Shows only your personally starred courses (count shown in the pill)

Left Sidebar Filters

Use the sidebar to narrow the visible dots in the scatter plot and the course list below:

  • Year — View a specific academic year or the all-time weighted average
  • Keywords — Searches course name, code, and instructor name
  • Concentration — Filter by academic area prefix (IGA, DPI, MLD, etc.)
  • Core Courses — Show all, core only, or electives only
  • Term — Filter to Fall, Spring, or January term (when a specific year is selected)
  • Min Instructor Rating — Hide courses below a percentile threshold
  • STEM — Filter to STEM A, STEM B, or both
  • Only courses with evals — Hide courses that have no evaluation data yet

Metric Display Toggle

Switch between two display modes using the sidebar toggle:

  • Score — Raw average score divided by 5, shown as a percentage (absolute measure)
  • Percentile — Rank vs. all courses in the dataset (relative measure) — useful for comparing instructors across years
All Years Average Selecting "All Years Average" in the Year filter computes weighted averages across all recorded offerings, weighting by number of respondents. Best for comparing instructors who have taught the same course multiple times.

3. Course Explorer — Detail View

The Courses page lets you search for a specific course and explore its full evaluation history, year-over-year trends, and bidding data.

Searching

Type a course name, code, or instructor into the search box. Results appear as you type. You can comma-separate multiple terms to match any of them (e.g., "Levy, climate, IGA").

Click any result to open the detail panel. The URL updates so you can bookmark or share a specific course.

Most Competitive Courses Panel

When no course is selected, a panel shows the top courses ranked by last clearing price. Each badge shows the clearing price in bid points. Higher = more competitive. This is useful for early-stage planning — if a course clears at 999 pts every semester, you need to commit accordingly.

Course Details Tab

The first tab shows two cards side by side:

Course Information

  • Academic area, STEM designation, Core/Cross-reg status
  • Course code, term, credits, grading basis
  • Schedule (days and times)
  • Enrollment bar — current enrolled vs. capacity (red if at capacity)
  • Instructor name (clickable — jumps to their Faculty profile)
  • Last Bid Snapshot — Clearing price, capacity, and number of bids from the most recent bidding round (see Section 6 for interpretation)

Student Experience

  • Instructor Rating — A label (Outstanding, Excellent, Good, Average, Poor) based on the percentile bracket, plus the raw score (0–5) and year median for context
  • Course Workload — A label (Very Light → Very Heavy) with the percentile and raw score. Workload at the 97th percentile means this course is more intensive than 97% of all HKS courses.

All Evaluation Metrics

Percentile bars for all 12 evaluation dimensions. Color coding:

ColorMeaning
Green≥ 75th percentile — top quartile
Amber25th–75th percentile — median range
Red≤ 25th percentile — bottom quartile
BlueWorkload and Rigor — higher isn't inherently better

Toggle "Show raw averages (0–5 scale)" below the bars to see the absolute scores.

Past Performance Tab

A line chart of key metrics across all semesters for which evaluation data exists. Use this to see whether a course has improved, declined, or stayed consistent over time. Multiple offerings in the same year (e.g., Spring and Fall) each appear as separate data points.

Bidding History Tab

A table of every semester with recorded bidding data, showing:

  • Clearing Price — Minimum bid that secured a seat
  • Capacity — Number of seats available
  • Bids — Number of students who bid
  • Oversubscribed — Whether bids exceeded capacity

Scroll right on mobile to see all columns.

Shortlist & Personal Notes

The "Add to Shortlist" button saves the course to your browser-stored shortlist. Shortlisted courses:

  • Appear with a ★ badge in the Course Cards list
  • Appear as gold outlined dots in the scatter plot
  • Can be compared side-by-side in the Compare tab
  • Are included in the Export CSV download from the nav panel

The My Notes field (below the action buttons) lets you write personal notes about any course. Notes save automatically to your browser and appear as a "Note" column when you export your shortlist to CSV.

Similar Courses

At the bottom of the Details tab, a Similar Courses section shows up to 6 courses that are semantically similar based on a PCA analysis of course descriptions and subject matter. Click any row to jump directly to that course's detail view.

4. Faculty Explorer

The Faculty page provides instructor-level profiles aggregating all their recorded course offerings.

Searching & Filtering

  • Search by name — Type any part of an instructor's name
  • Sort — Name A–Z, Instructor Rating (desc), Course Rating (desc), Most Courses taught, Most Respondents
  • Concentration — Filter by academic area
  • Min Instructor Rating — Hide instructors below a percentile threshold
  • Min Courses — Show only instructors with N or more courses with evaluation data
  • Active Since — Hide instructors who haven't taught since a given year. Set to 2024 or 2025 to find currently active faculty.

Average Ratings

Once you click an instructor, the detail panel shows percentile bars for all evaluation dimensions, weighted across all their courses. Color coding is identical to the Course Details view (green/amber/red, with blue for Workload and Rigor).

Quick Stats

The Quick Stats card shows:

  • Headline Instructor Rating % — The instructor's overall percentile across all their offerings
  • avg X.XX/5 — The raw 0–5 average score (absolute, not relative)
  • all-courses med X.XX — The median raw score across all courses in the entire dataset — so you can see whether this instructor's raw score is above or below the typical HKS course, independently of percentile shifts over time
  • Sub-stats for Course Rating, Workload, Rigor, Diverse Perspectives, and Feedback
Tip — Why raw scores matter A 92nd percentile instructor rating with a 4.85/5 raw score is very strong. A 92nd percentile rating with a 4.30/5 raw score tells a different story — it means the entire cohort scored lower that year. Always check both the percentile and the raw score.

Full Teaching History

A table of every course this instructor has taught with evaluation data. Click any row to jump directly to that course's detail page in the Course Explorer. Columns include: Year, Term, Course code & name, Instructor %, Course %, Workload %, Rigor %, Diverse Perspectives %, and N (number of respondents).

5. Compare Tab

The Compare tab lets you stack up to 5 shortlisted courses side by side across all evaluation dimensions and bidding history.

Building Your Shortlist

  1. Navigate to any course in the Course Explorer
  2. Click "Add to Shortlist" (star button at the bottom of the detail panel)
  3. Repeat for up to 5 courses
  4. Switch to the Compare tab

Reading the Comparison Table

Each column represents one shortlisted course. Rows show evaluation metrics with the same color coding as the rest of the app. The last row shows the most recent clearing price for quick bidding comparison.

Remove a course from the comparison by clicking the × next to its name.

Tip Shortlist data is stored in your browser's localStorage. It persists across sessions on the same device but is not synced across devices.

6. Schedule Builder

The Schedule Builder lets you assemble a semester timetable visually, detect conflicts, track total credits, and export a calendar file. It works in parallel with the Requirements Tracker — courses added here automatically feed your degree progress.

Desktop only The Schedule Builder requires a large screen for the timetable grid. On mobile you will see a redirect notice.

Search & Add Courses

Use the left panel to search for courses:

  • Type any course name, code, or instructor in the search box
  • Filter by Concentration, STEM, Core only, Semester, and Source (HKS / Non-HKS / All)
  • Click Add to add a course to the current plan's shortlist
  • Your starred courses from Home appear automatically in the From Your Shortlist section for quick access

Four Parallel Plans

Plans A, B, C and D let you model entirely different semester scenarios simultaneously — for example, Plan A = Spring heavy, Plan B = Fall heavy. Switch plans at any time from the top bar without losing any plan's data. Each plan stores its own course list, grid placement, and completed courses independently.

Visual Timetable Grid

Once a course is in the shortlist, click the grid icon (or "Done" button) on the course card to place it on the timetable. Meeting times come from two sources:

  • Supabase database — Pre-loaded times from the HKS course schedule for current-semester HKS courses
  • Harvard Course API — Live data for courses found in the API

If a course has no time data yet, you'll see a "No schedule data" message. You can still keep it in your shortlist.

Conflict Detection

Overlapping courses are highlighted in red on the grid. A Conflicts section appears in the right panel listing all conflicting pairs.

Semester & Term Filters

Use the semester quick-chips (Spring / Fall / J-term) to filter the course search and section times database to the relevant semester. The Q1 / Q2 / Full Term buttons affect the iCal export start dates only.

Plan Shortlist (Right Panel)

The right panel shows all courses in the current plan with:

  • Total credit count
  • Avg percentile ratings across Instructor, Course, Workload, and Rigor
  • Individual credit counts and grid status per course
  • The Requirements Tracker below the shortlist (see Section 7)

Completed Courses

Enter past course codes in the Completed box (right panel) to mark them as already done. Completed courses count toward your Requirements progress automatically. Type a code and press Enter or click Add.

Export to iCal

Click Export iCal to download a .ics calendar file for all courses currently on the grid with schedule data. Import it into Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook. The file repeats weekly for 14 weeks (Full Term) or 7 weeks (Q1/Q2).

Copy Plan as Text

Click 📋 Copy Plan to copy a formatted text summary of your plan to the clipboard — useful for sharing with advisors, study groups, or pasting into a note.

7. Requirements Tracker

The Requirements Tracker calculates your degree progress in real time based on courses saved in your Schedule Builder plan. It supports all five HKS programs.

Supported Programs

ProgramTotal Credits
MPA — 2-Year64 credits
MPA-ID (International Development)64 credits
MC/MPA (Mid-Career)48 credits
MPP (Public Policy)48 credits
MC/MPP (Mid-Career Public Policy)48 credits

How Progress is Calculated

The tracker reads courses from the Schedule Builder plan you select (A, B, C, or D) and matches each course to the requirements it satisfies — using concentration prefix, STEM designation, and Core flag. The same course can satisfy multiple requirements (e.g., a STEM course also counts for its distribution requirement).

Requirement Categories

Each program has these categories (with program-specific variations):

  • Distribution Requirements (Economics & QA, Management, Ethics & Political Institutions) — at least 4 credits each, no double-counting with PAC
  • Policy Area of Concentration (PAC) — 8 credits in one policy area (BGP, DPI, IGA, DEV, or SUP prefix)
  • STEM Designation — 16 STEM-designated credits for the 24-month OPT extension. STEM courses are non-exclusive: they count toward STEM and another category simultaneously
  • Electives — Remaining credits to reach the program total
STEM OPT Tip If you need the 24-month STEM OPT extension, you need 16 credits of STEM-designated courses. These are identified with STEM-A or STEM-B tags in the course catalog. Use the STEM filter in the Course Explorer to find them. They are non-exclusive — they count toward STEM and your other requirements simultaneously.

Finding Completing Courses

Each requirement card shows a "Find completing courses" button that opens the Course Explorer filtered to courses that can satisfy that category. The "Open in Schedule Builder →" button jumps directly to the Schedule Builder with the relevant search pre-loaded.

Plan Sync

The Requirements Tracker and Schedule Builder share the same four plans (A, B, C, D). Changes you make in the Schedule Builder immediately reflect in the Requirements Tracker when you switch to that tab. The URL includes the plan parameter (?p=MPA_2YR&plan=A) so you can bookmark any state.

8. HUB Style — Institutional View

HUB Style switches the app's navigation and visual design to match the Harvard institutional aesthetic — a full-width horizontal top navigation bar in Harvard Crimson, white panels, sharp corners, and Georgia serif headings.

How to Switch

  • Activate: Click 🏛 HUB Style at the bottom of the left sidebar (Classic mode)
  • Deactivate: Click ← Classic View in the top-right of the Hub navigation bar
  • Your preference is saved to your browser and persists across sessions and page navigations

What Changes in HUB Style

  • Navigation — The left sidebar is replaced by a full-width top bar with the HKS shield logo, all six page links, and action buttons on the right
  • Colors — Harvard Crimson (#C41230) replaces the custom accent color; backgrounds shift to clean white and light gray
  • Typography — Arial body font; Georgia for headings — matching Harvard's institutional style guide
  • Corners — All rounded elements become sharp-cornered (2–4px radius) consistent with Harvard web properties
  • Content — Page content is unchanged; all filters, data, and interactions work identically

When to Use HUB Style

HUB Style is designed for presentations, shared screens, and situations where the tool's Harvard affiliation should be visually evident. The Classic dark/light design is optimized for extended personal use during bidding season research.

9. Understanding Bidding Data

HKS uses a bid-point auction system for course selection. Understanding the data helps you allocate your points strategically.

Key Terms

TermDefinition
Clearing PriceThe minimum bid that secured a seat in that round. Every student who bid at or above this amount got in. Anyone who bid below did not.
CapacityNumber of seats available in that round.
BidsNumber of students who submitted a bid for the course.
OversubscribedBids exceeded capacity. Not everyone who bid secured a seat — only those at or above the clearing price.

How to Use This Data

  • Check the Bidding History tab to see whether a course consistently clears high or has become more/less competitive over time.
  • A course that cleared at 50–100 pts historically is unlikely to require 500 pts this cycle unless circumstances change.
  • A course that was oversubscribed every semester at a high clearing price is a "high-risk" bid — you may need to commit a large portion of your points.
  • Courses that were not oversubscribed (bids ≤ capacity) often cleared at a nominal price (sometimes as low as 1 pt) — these are safer to bid on with fewer points.
  • The Most Competitive Courses panel on the Courses page ranks by last clearing price for quick reference.
Caution Bidding dynamics can shift significantly year to year based on cohort size, competing courses, and instructor reputation. Historical data is a guide, not a guarantee. Use it alongside conversations with classmates and your academic advisor.

Example: IGA-211 (Graham Allison)

IGA-211 has historically cleared at 999 pts — the maximum. This means it is consistently the most demanded course in the catalog. If you want a seat, you need to bid your entire point allocation. The bidding history tab will show whether this pattern held every semester, helping you decide if it's worth the commitment.

10. Evaluation Metrics Explained

All metrics come from HKS end-of-term student evaluations (QReports). Each is rated on a 1–5 scale by enrolled students and then converted to a percentile rank vs. all courses in the dataset.

MetricWhat it measuresNote
Instructor RatingOverall quality of the instructor — preparation, engagement, clarityThe primary faculty quality signal
Course RatingOverall satisfaction with the course itself, independent of instructorReflects curriculum design, relevance, and intellectual value
WorkloadVolume of work required — readings, assignments, projectsHigher = heavier workload. Blue bars — neutral
RigorIntellectual and analytical difficultyHigher = more demanding. Blue bars — neutral
Assignment ValueWhether assignments contributed meaningfully to learning
ReadingsQuality and relevance of assigned readings
FeedbackQuality and timeliness of feedback from the instructor
Class DiscussionsQuality of in-class discussion and student participation
Diverse PerspectivesInclusion of diverse viewpoints and representation in course content
Discussion DiversityRange of student voices and backgrounds in discussions
AvailabilityInstructor availability outside class (office hours, email)
InsightsWhether the course provided new insights or changed how students thinkHigh-level intellectual impact
N (Respondents) Metrics based on fewer than ~10 respondents should be treated with caution — they are more volatile. The N value is shown on every course and faculty card.

11. Tips & Settings

Guided Tours

An interactive spotlight tour auto-starts on your first visit to each section (Home, Courses, Faculty, Compare, Schedule Builder, Requirements). Each tour highlights key UI elements with a spotlight effect and explains how to use them.

To replay any tour:

  • Click the ❓ Tour button in the top navigation bar (Hub mode) or at the bottom of the sidebar (Classic mode)
  • Or click "↺ Replay tour" in the left sidebar filter panel on any page

Colorblind Mode

Available in the sidebar filter panel under Accessibility. Replaces the default red/green color scheme with orange/blue for all scatter plot quadrant shading.

Light / Dark Mode

Toggle between dark and light themes using the button in the top-left of the navigation panel (desktop) or the top-right of the mobile header. Your preference is saved to your browser.

Shareable URLs

All pages sync their state to the URL automatically. You can share any URL to reproduce an exact view:

  • Course detail — /courses?id=COURSE_ID
  • Faculty profile — /faculty?prof=Name
  • Compare view — /compare?ids=MLD-601,DPI-622 (up to 5 comma-separated codes)
  • Home filters — Year, concentration, STEM, core, sort, scatter axes all persist in the URL
  • Shortlist share — The "Share Shortlist" button copies a link that pre-loads your starred courses for anyone who opens it

Shortlist Persistence & Export

Your shortlist (starred courses) is stored in browser localStorage. It persists across sessions on the same device. Clearing your browser data will reset it.

Click "⬇ Export CSV" in the top navigation to download your entire shortlist as a spreadsheet. The export includes: course code, title, instructor, year, term, concentration, STEM/Core flags, key metric percentiles, last bid price, and any personal notes you've written.

AI Course Advisor

A chat interface (💬 button in the bottom-right corner) lets you ask questions about courses in natural language. It uses the 2025 dataset and free AI models — treat it as a starting point, not a definitive source. Good uses: "Which core courses are lightest workload?", "Who teaches climate policy?", "What's the difference between IGA-101 and IGA-215?"

Best practice for bidding season Build your shortlist of 5–8 target courses in the Compare tab. Check their bidding history to estimate required point commitments. Then rank them by priority and allocate points accordingly, keeping some in reserve for Round 2. Export your shortlist as a CSV to share with study groups or your academic advisor.
